Market Analysis and Performance Indicators

Introduction

This guide provides detailed explanations of the key metrics and insights used in our market analysis dashboards. It is designed to help decision-makers understand the technical aspects of our market performance indicators and their business implications.

Each metric is explained in terms of:

  • What it measures
  • How it's calculated
  • How to interpret different values
  • Business implications and actionable insights
  • Practical examples where applicable

The metrics are organized into categories that align with our dashboard structure, making it easy to reference when reviewing market performance.

Brand Health Metrics

Brand health metrics measure consumer perceptions and attitudes toward our destination. These metrics are leading indicators that typically precede changes in actual travel behavior.

Awareness

67.12% (+3.12pp)

Awareness measures the percentage of the target audience who are aware of Abu Dhabi as a Cultural and Sea & Sun travel destination.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which international holiday destinations offering a combination of both cultural and sun & beach activities are you aware of?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of the total surveyed population.

Interpretation:
  • 90%+: Excellent awareness, approaching market saturation
  • 70-90%: Strong awareness, but room for improvement
  • 50-70%: Moderate awareness, significant opportunity for growth
  • <50%: Low awareness, major opportunity but requires substantial investment
Business Implications:
  • At 67.12%, Romania has moderate awareness of Abu Dhabi, indicating good market penetration but significant room for growth.
  • The +3.12pp year-over-year growth suggests continued improvement in awareness levels.
  • This moderate awareness level positions Romania in the upper range of the "moderate awareness" category, suggesting that continued investment in awareness-building activities could yield significant returns.
  • The positive trend in awareness provides a solid foundation for more targeted marketing efforts aimed at improving consideration and intent metrics.
  • Given the relatively high awareness but low intent (6.64%), marketing efforts should focus on addressing specific barriers that prevent awareness from converting to consideration and intent.

Consideration

19.45% (+3.45pp)

Consideration measures the percentage of aware consumers who would consider travelling to Abu Dhabi in the next 3 years.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations would you like to visit in the next 3 years?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are aware of Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 70%+: Excellent consideration, strong position in consumer mindset
  • 50-70%: Good consideration, competitive position
  • 30-50%: Moderate consideration, significant barriers exist
  • <30%: Low consideration, major perception issues to address
Business Implications:
  • At 19.45%, consideration is in the low range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from awareness to consideration.
  • The +3.45pp increase shows a positive trend that should be leveraged.
  • This suggests a significant gap between awareness (67.12%) and consideration (19.45%), indicating that while many Romanian travelers know about Abu Dhabi, far fewer are actively considering it as a destination.
  • Marketing should focus on addressing the specific barriers that prevent aware consumers from considering Abu Dhabi, particularly the perception of restrictive laws/customs (52%) and unfriendly people (36%).
  • The positive growth in consideration (+3.45pp) despite these barriers suggests that current marketing efforts are having some success and should be continued while addressing specific concerns.

Intent

6.64% (-0.36pp)

Intent measures the percentage of considering consumers who intend to visit Abu Dhabi in the next 12 months.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations are you likely to visit in the next 12 months?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are considering Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 50%+: Excellent intent, very strong conversion potential
  • 30-50%: Good intent, healthy conversion potential
  • 20-30%: Moderate intent, conversion challenges exist
  • <20%: Low intent, significant conversion barriers
Business Implications:
  • At 6.64%, intent is in the very low range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from consideration to intent.
  • The -0.36pp decrease shows a concerning negative trend that requires immediate attention.
  • This suggests that even among those who consider Abu Dhabi, very few have immediate plans to visit.
  • The decline in intent (-0.36pp) despite growth in awareness and consideration indicates increasing competition or emerging barriers that are affecting the Romanian market specifically.
  • Marketing should focus on creating urgency and addressing practical barriers to booking (visa process, flight availability, etc.).
  • The low intent level with negative growth suggests that tactical, conversion-focused campaigns are urgently needed to reverse this trend.

Intent/Consideration Ratio

0.34 (-0.10)

This ratio measures the efficiency of converting consideration to intent, indicating how effectively we move consumers down the funnel.

How It's Calculated:

Intent percentage divided by Consideration percentage. For example: 6.64% ÷ 19.45% = 0.34

Interpretation:
  • 0.8+: Excellent conversion efficiency, minimal drop-off
  • 0.6-0.8: Good conversion efficiency
  • 0.4-0.6: Moderate conversion efficiency, significant drop-off
  • <0.4: Poor conversion efficiency, major funnel leakage
Business Implications:
  • At 0.34, the conversion from consideration to intent is poor, indicating that only 34% of those who consider Abu Dhabi intend to visit in the next 12 months.
  • The decrease of -0.10 suggests a significant deterioration in conversion efficiency.
  • This indicates that while consideration is growing, intent is declining, resulting in a widening gap between these metrics.
  • The poor ratio suggests that once Romanian travelers consider Abu Dhabi, they face significant barriers that prevent them from developing actual intent to visit.
  • Marketing should focus on understanding and addressing the specific barriers that are preventing consideration from converting to intent, particularly the perception of restrictive laws/customs (52%) and unfriendly people (36%).
  • The significant decline in this ratio (-0.10) is a concerning sign that requires immediate attention through targeted marketing campaigns that address specific barriers to conversion.

Market Characteristics

Market characteristics describe fundamental travel behaviors and patterns specific to the Romania market. These metrics help inform tactical marketing decisions and campaign timing.

Average Length of Stay

3.1 days

The average number of nights Romanian travelers spend in Abu Dhabi per visit.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel guest data and visitor surveys (DVS), calculated as the sum of all nights stayed divided by the number of visitors.

Business Implications:
  • The 3.1-day average stay indicates a short vacation timeframe, typical of many European markets.
  • This suggests marketing should focus on compact itineraries and experiences that can be enjoyed within a 3-day timeframe.
  • Package offerings should be designed around this timeframe to maximize appeal.
  • There's an opportunity to develop weekend-plus packages that include Abu Dhabi as a short break destination.
  • Hotel partners should be encouraged to develop 3-day packages with all-inclusive options to appeal to this market.
  • The relatively short stay suggests a need for high-impact experiences that deliver maximum value in a limited time.

Booking Window

67 days

The average time between booking and arrival for Romanian travelers visiting Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on booking data from hotels, flight booking engines and OTAs, calculated as the average number of days between booking confirmation and arrival date.

Business Implications:
  • The 67-day booking window is relatively long, indicating a market that plans trips well in advance.
  • Marketing campaigns should be timed to run approximately 2-3 months before target travel periods.
  • This window allows for considered decision-making and comparison shopping by travelers.
  • Early booking incentives are likely to be effective for this market.
  • The long booking window suggests that both digital and traditional media can be effective, as there is sufficient time for consumers to respond to marketing messages.
  • This extended planning period also provides opportunities for upselling and package enhancements over time.
Strategic Example:

For the October-November peak season, major campaign pushes should begin in August to align with the 67-day booking window. Early booking discounts offered 80+ days in advance could be particularly effective for this market, encouraging even earlier commitments.

Peak Months (Hotel Guests)

Aug & Sep

The months with the highest volume of Romanian hotel guests in Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel occupancy data, identifying the months with the highest number of Romanian guests.

Business Implications:
  • August (4,724 guests) and September (4,685 guests) represent the clear peak season for Romanian travelers, with October (4,209) as a close third.
  • This autumn peak aligns with favorable weather conditions and Romanian holiday periods.
  • Marketing should ensure sufficient inventory and capacity during these months.
  • Pricing strategies can be optimized for these high-demand periods.
  • There's an opportunity to develop shoulder season campaigns (July, November) to extend the peak periods.
  • Summer months (June-July) represent a significant growth opportunity through targeted campaigns highlighting indoor activities and special summer offers.

Advanced Statistical Metrics

These advanced metrics provide deeper insights into market dynamics, helping to quantify relationships between marketing activities and outcomes, forecast accuracy, and market behavior patterns.

Seasonality Index

2.1 (moderate-high seasonal variation)

A statistical measure that quantifies the degree of seasonal variation in the Romania travel market data.

How It's Calculated:

Calculated by dividing the highest monthly value by the average monthly value in a year. For the Romania market, the 2025 forecast data shows November as the highest month (8.41) with an average monthly value of 4.03, resulting in a seasonality index of approximately 2.1.

Interpretation:
  • 1.0-1.3: Low seasonal variation (relatively flat demand throughout the year)
  • 1.3-1.6: Moderate seasonal variation (noticeable peaks and troughs)
  • 1.6-2.0: High seasonal variation (significant peaks and troughs)
  • 2.0+: Extreme seasonal variation (very pronounced peaks and low seasons)
Business Implications:
  • The 2.1 value indicates that the market has extreme seasonal fluctuations, with peak months having about 110% higher travel query activity than the average month.
  • This is significant enough to warrant strong seasonal marketing strategies.
  • Marketing efforts should be concentrated before peak periods (considering the 67-day booking window).
  • There's also opportunity to grow travel interest during off-peak months through targeted campaigns.
  • Resource allocation should follow this seasonal pattern, with increased investment before peak periods.
  • The high seasonality suggests a need for differential pricing and promotion strategies across the year.

Media Elasticity

0.38 (moderate impact)

A marketing metric that measures how responsive travel queries are to changes in media impressions for the Romania market.

How It's Calculated:

Calculated using regression analysis on historical data, comparing the percentage change in travel queries to the percentage change in media impressions over time.

Formula: Media Elasticity = % Change in Travel Queries ÷ % Change in Media Impressions

Interpretation:
  • <0.3: Low impact (minimal response to media investment)
  • 0.3-0.6: Moderate impact (reasonable response to media investment)
  • 0.6-1.0: High impact (strong response to media investment)
  • >1.0: Very high impact (exceptional response to media investment)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.38 elasticity means that for every 10% increase in media impressions, travel queries increase by approximately 3.8%.
  • This indicates that media investments drive travel queries with moderate efficiency.
  • The relationship is positive and in the middle of the moderate range (0.3-0.6).
  • Media campaigns will generate positive ROI but with moderate efficiency.
  • Doubling media spend will not double travel queries, but will produce modest growth.
  • The moderate elasticity suggests that the Romania market is somewhat responsive to media campaigns, but other factors also significantly influence travel decisions.
  • This moderate responsiveness suggests that media investments should be complemented by other marketing approaches, such as addressing specific barriers to visitation.
Strategic Example:

If you're planning to increase media impressions by 20% for a campaign, you can expect approximately a 7.6% increase in travel queries (20% × 0.38 = 7.6%). This helps set realistic expectations for campaign performance and ROI calculations.

YoY Growth Consistency

0.76 (moderate consistency)

A statistical measure that evaluates how uniform or predictable the year-over-year growth patterns are for the Romania travel market.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated using a statistical measure like the coefficient of variation (inverse) or correlation coefficient of YoY growth rates across different time periods. The scale ranges from 0 to 1, where 0 represents completely inconsistent growth (highly volatile) and 1 represents perfectly consistent growth (same growth rate across all periods).

Interpretation:
  • <0.5: Low consistency (high volatility, unpredictable patterns)
  • 0.5-0.7: Moderate consistency (some predictability with variations)
  • 0.7-0.9: High consistency (reliable patterns with minor variations)
  • >0.9: Very high consistency (highly predictable patterns)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.76 consistency indicates that there is high predictability in how the market behaves year-over-year.
  • Forecasting can be done with good confidence.
  • Most months will show similar growth patterns to previous years.
  • Only occasional months may show unexpected variations requiring adaptive strategies.
  • Marketing teams can plan with reasonable certainty about expected outcomes.
  • Historical patterns provide reliable guidance for future planning.
  • This high consistency (0.76) contributes to a relatively narrow forecast confidence interval of ±7.3%.

Forecast Confidence Interval

±7.3%

A statistical measure indicating the range within which we expect the actual 2025 travel query values to fall with a certain level of confidence (typically 95%).

How It's Calculated:

Derived from statistical analysis of historical forecast accuracy, market volatility, and model parameters. It represents the standard error of the forecast multiplied by a factor corresponding to the desired confidence level (typically 1.96 for 95% confidence).

Interpretation:
  • <5%: Very high forecast precision, highly reliable predictions
  • 5-10%: Good forecast precision, reliable predictions with moderate variance
  • 10-15%: Moderate forecast precision, usable predictions with significant variance
  • >15%: Low forecast precision, high uncertainty in predictions
Business Implications:
  • The ±7.3% confidence interval means that if we forecast a travel query index of 150.0 for a particular month, the actual value is expected to fall between 139.1 and 160.9 with 95% confidence.
  • This relatively narrow interval indicates a reliable forecast for the Romania market.
  • Marketing teams should plan with appropriate contingencies, knowing the potential range of outcomes.
  • Budget allocations should include flexibility to adjust based on actual performance within this range.
  • The confidence interval suggests that forecasts can be used with good confidence for planning purposes.

Barriers to Visitation

Understanding why potential visitors choose not to travel to Abu Dhabi is crucial for developing targeted marketing strategies that address specific concerns and misconceptions.

Top Barriers to Visit

5 key factors

The primary reasons Romanian travelers give for not considering Abu Dhabi as a travel destination, based on survey research.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "What are the main reasons you would not consider traveling to Abu Dhabi?" The percentages represent the proportion of respondents who selected each reason.

Current Barriers (Q4 2024):
  • Restrictive Laws/Customs: 52% (-2pp) find laws/customs too restrictive
  • Unfriendly People: 36% (+4pp) heard people are not friendly/helpful
  • Safety Concerns: 31% (-1pp) perceive it as unsafe
  • Destination Preference: 31% (-2pp) prefer other destinations
  • Expense Concerns: 24% (-1pp) think it's too expensive
Business Implications:
  • Restrictive Laws/Customs (52%, -2pp): Despite a slight decrease, this remains the dominant barrier by a significant margin. Marketing should emphasize recent liberalization of laws and customs in Abu Dhabi.
  • Unfriendly People (36%, +4pp): This concerning increase suggests growing negative perceptions about local hospitality. Marketing should showcase positive interactions between locals and visitors.
  • Safety Concerns (31%, -1pp): Despite a slight decrease, safety remains a significant concern. Marketing should emphasize Abu Dhabi's strong safety record and low crime rates.
  • Destination Preference (31%, -2pp): The slight decrease suggests improving competitive positioning. Marketing should continue to differentiate Abu Dhabi from competing destinations.
  • Expense Concerns (24%, -1pp): The slight decrease suggests improving value perception. Marketing should continue to emphasize value propositions and affordable options.
Strategic Recommendation:

Develop a "Modern Abu Dhabi" campaign specifically for the Romanian market that addresses the top barrier of restrictive laws/customs (52%). This campaign should feature Romanian travelers enjoying Abu Dhabi's cosmopolitan lifestyle, highlighting the ease of visiting and the welcoming nature of locals (addressing the unfriendly people perception at 36%). Include content that showcases Abu Dhabi's safety, modern infrastructure, and security measures to address safety concerns (31%).

Conclusion

This guide provides a comprehensive explanation of the key metrics used in our market analysis for Romania. By understanding these metrics and their business implications, DCT - Abu Dhabi can make more informed strategic marketing decisions.

The Romania market shows several distinctive characteristics:

  • Moderate awareness (67.12%) but low consideration (19.45%) and very low intent (6.64%), indicating significant barriers to conversion
  • A poor intent-to-consideration ratio (0.34) indicating significant funnel leakage once consideration is achieved
  • A relatively short average stay (3.1 days) indicating short-break vacation patterns
  • A long booking window (67 days) allowing for effective campaign planning
  • Distinct autumn seasonality with peaks in August-October for hotel guests and October-November for travel queries
  • Moderate media elasticity (0.38) suggesting reasonable but not strong response to media investments
  • Strong interest in cultural attractions, with Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque and Saadiyat Beach being the most searched attractions
  • Preference for centrally located accommodations near cultural attractions
  • Significant barriers including restrictive laws/customs perception (52%), unfriendly people perception (36%), and safety concerns (31%)
  • Concerning growth in no direct flights (+7pp), unfriendly people perception (+4pp), and language barriers (+4pp)

These insights suggest a strategic focus on:

  • Addressing the significant gap between awareness and consideration through targeted messaging that addresses specific barriers
  • Countering the perception of restrictive laws/customs through content that highlights Abu Dhabi's modern lifestyle and recent liberalization of laws
  • Addressing the growing perception of unfriendly people through testimonials and content that showcases positive interactions between locals and visitors
  • Developing targeted campaigns to address safety concerns, highlighting Abu Dhabi's strong safety record and security measures
  • Creating marketing content that showcases the blend of cultural attractions, beaches, and entertainment options available in Abu Dhabi
  • Developing partnerships with centrally located hotels that offer easy access to cultural attractions
  • Timing campaigns to align with the 67-day booking window, particularly for the August-October peak period
  • Addressing the growing concern about lack of direct flights through airline partnerships or improved connectivity messaging
  • Developing Romanian language support materials to address growing language barrier concerns
  • Creating compact itineraries that maximize the typical 3.1-day stay, focusing on high-impact cultural experiences and beach relaxation
  • Emphasizing the diversity of experiences available to maximize engagement during the short average stay duration

With these insights and ongoing monitoring of these metrics, DCT - Abu Dhabi can formulate more effective marketing strategies and increase our return on marketing investment in the Romania market. The moderate media elasticity (0.38) suggests that media investments will drive travel queries with reasonable efficiency, particularly when addressing the specific barriers identified in this analysis.
